# Ledger Feature Archetypes

Use this reference when a Ledger feature needs a strong theme, progression
fantasy, and screen structure. Treat each section as a design kit, not a rigid
spec. Adapt to the existing game state, tabs, and save format.

## 1. Archetype Rules

Every major Ledger expansion should have a fantasy sentence.

Examples:

- Crime: "Climb from petty jobs to organized power while managing heat."
- Boxing: "Train, fight, recover, and chase legacy before your body breaks."
- Royalty: "Use ceremony, marriage, court influence, and succession to build a dynasty."
- Vampire: "Balance hunger, secrecy, power, and ancient politics."
- Billionaire: "Allocate capital, control institutions, and convert wealth into legacy."

Turn the fantasy sentence into five UX pillars:

- Identity: what the player is.
- Resource: what the player spends.
- Pressure: what threatens them.
- Ladder: how they advance.
- Consequence: what changes after choices.

Do not build a feature with only actions and payouts.

## 2. Crime Expansion

### Fantasy

Crime should feel like pressure, risk, and escalation. The player starts with
small illegal choices and may grow into a crew, enterprise, or shadow empire.

### First Screen

Show:

- Criminal rank.
- Cash from illicit sources.
- Heat.
- Crew loyalty.
- Territory.
- Active job.
- Legal exposure.
- Next escalation milestone.

### Primary Metrics

- Heat: police attention and surveillance.
- Crew Loyalty: chance of betrayal, cooperation, and job quality.
- Reputation: fear/respect in the underworld.
- Territory: neighborhood or market control.
- Clean Money: laundered spendable money.
- Dirty Money: cash that creates risk until laundered.
- Legal Defense: attorney/accountant protection.
- Rival Pressure: chance of ambush, undercutting, or retaliation.

### Progression Ladder

- Petty thief.
- Runner.
- Crew member.
- Specialist.
- Enforcer.
- Lieutenant.
- Boss.
- Syndicate figure.
- Shadow power.

### Action Groups

Use these sections:

- Jobs: pickpocket, burglary, smuggling run, fraud scheme, heist.
- Crew: recruit, pay crew, discipline crew, promote specialist.
- Territory: claim block, negotiate, intimidate rival, defend turf.
- Laundering: clean cash, buy front business, pay accountant.
- Legal: hire attorney, reduce heat, prepare alibi.
- Power: bribe official, acquire influence, install proxy.

### UX Feel

The screen should feel tense but readable.

Use warning color for heat.

Show job risk before the player clicks.

Use short consequence text:

- "The job paid, but the getaway was sloppy."
- "Crew loyalty rises after a generous split."
- "A rival sees your expansion as a threat."

### Risk Events

Use modal choices for major moments:

- Police raid.
- Informant discovered.
- Rival crew attacks.
- Big score opportunity.
- Money laundering audit.
- Family member asks questions.

Each choice should have:

- Cost.
- Heat impact.
- Loyalty impact.
- Money impact.
- Possible long-term flag.

### Refined Presentation

Do not make Crime a plain list of illegal buttons.

Give the player a "crew board" or "operation desk":

- Top row: rank, heat, loyalty, dirty money.
- Main panel: active opportunity.
- Side panel: crew and territory.
- Lower panel: recent consequences.

## 3. Monster Expansion

### Fantasy

Monster gameplay should feel like two lives colliding: ordinary life and hidden
inhuman pressure.

### First Screen

Show:

- Human identity status.
- Monster type or transformation stage.
- Hunger/rage/instinct.
- Secrecy.
- Hunter attention.
- Safehouse/lair.
- Current weakness.
- Next transformation milestone.

### Primary Metrics

- Hunger or Rage.
- Control.
- Secrecy.
- Humanity.
- Power.
- Hunter Attention.
- Lair Safety.
- Public Suspicion.

### Progression Ladder

- Unaware.
- Changed.
- Unstable.
- Controlled.
- Feared.
- Alpha or master.
- Myth.

### Action Groups

- Feed or recover.
- Hide evidence.
- Train control.
- Improve lair.
- Use power.
- Manage witnesses.
- Confront hunters.
- Seek cure or embrace.

### UX Feel

Use a split identity layout:

- Left: normal life consequences.
- Right: monster pressure.

The feature should not feel like only a power fantasy. It should create tradeoffs.

### Risk Events

- Loss of control.
- Witness encounter.
- Hunter trap.
- Loved one threatened.
- Full moon / blood moon / curse surge.
- Public video leak.

### Refined Presentation

Use status chips that carry narrative weight:

- "Control: Fraying"
- "Secrecy: Stable"
- "Hunter Trail: Warm"
- "Humanity: Fading"

## 4. Vampire Expansion

### Fantasy

Vampires should feel elegant, predatory, political, and secretive. The player
manages blood, the Masquerade, coven politics, powers, thralls, and hunters.

### First Screen

Show:

- Vampire age/rank.
- Blood.
- Masquerade risk.
- Coven standing.
- Territory.
- Thralls.
- Hunter attention.
- Active scheme.
- Next status milestone.

### Primary Metrics

- Blood.
- Masquerade.
- Coven Standing.
- Power.
- Territory.
- Thralls.
- Hunter Heat.
- Humanity.
- Age.

### Progression Ladder

- Mortal contact.
- Ghoul.
- Newly turned.
- Neonate.
- Recognized vampire.
- Harpy or enforcer.
- Coven officer.
- Elder.
- Prince.
- Ancient.

### Action Groups

- Feed: discreet hunt, social feeding, risky feeding, blood bank.
- Masquerade: clean traces, silence witness, plant cover story.
- Coven: attend court, trade favor, challenge rival, sponsor ghoul.
- Powers: train discipline, use charm, use shadow, dominate target.
- Territory: claim venue, protect haven, expand domain.
- Hunters: misdirect, negotiate, ambush, flee.

### UX Feel

Use courtly language.

Make danger feel refined:

- "The court notices your restraint."
- "A hunter marks your feeding pattern."
- "Your thrall solves the problem, but asks for more blood."

### Risk Events

- Masquerade breach.
- Hunter investigation.
- Rival elder insult.
- Blood shortage.
- Thrall betrayal.
- Invitation to court.
- Forbidden romance.

### Refined Presentation

Recommended layout:

- Header: title/rank and night status.
- Blood/Masquerade strip.
- Court panel.
- Feeding panel.
- Powers panel.
- Hunter warning feed.

Avoid horror clutter. Keep it sharp, dark, and prestigious.

## 5. Royalty Expansion

### Fantasy

Royalty should feel like ceremony, status, inheritance, public approval, and
private manipulation.

### First Screen

Show:

- Title.
- Line of succession.
- Court influence.
- Public approval.
- Estate wealth.
- Marriage/alliance status.
- Rival claimant.
- Current scandal or ceremony.

### Primary Metrics

- Title Rank.
- Influence.
- Public Approval.
- Court Favor.
- Treasury/Estate Income.
- Succession Strength.
- Scandal.
- Alliance Power.
- Rival Claim.

### Progression Ladder

- Court outsider.
- Minor noble.
- Favored courtier.
- Baron/Baroness.
- Count/Countess.
- Duke/Duchess.
- Regent.
- Monarch.
- Dynasty founder.

### Action Groups

- Court: attend court, host banquet, flatter monarch, expose rival.
- Dynasty: arrange marriage, educate heir, secure succession.
- Estate: improve lands, collect rents, commission palace.
- Public: charity tour, speech, military parade, scandal response.
- Intrigue: spy, blackmail, alliance, disinherit rival.
- Ceremony: coronation, investiture, royal wedding, state funeral.

### UX Feel

Use hierarchy and ceremony.

The player should feel watched.

Use "public" and "private" panels:

- Public legitimacy.
- Private influence.

### Risk Events

- Succession crisis.
- Scandal leak.
- Rival claimant.
- Marriage dispute.
- Estate debt.
- Public protest.
- Royal favor withdrawn.

### Refined Presentation

The top of the screen should feel like a court dossier:

- Title badge.
- Coat-of-arms style icon if available.
- Succession chip.
- Approval chip.
- Rival chip.

Do not bury succession. Succession is the drama.

## 6. Boxing Expansion

### Fantasy

Boxing should feel physical, seasonal, risky, and legacy-driven. The player
trains, books fights, manages injuries, builds fame, and chases belts.

### First Screen

Show:

- Record.
- Weight class.
- Rank.
- Fitness.
- Technique.
- Power.
- Injury risk.
- Upcoming fight.
- Purse.
- Title path.

### Primary Metrics

- Fitness.
- Technique.
- Power.
- Defense.
- Chin.
- Cut/Injury Risk.
- Weight.
- Ranking.
- Fame.
- Legacy.

### Progression Ladder

- Amateur.
- Prospect.
- Regional contender.
- National contender.
- World title challenger.
- Champion.
- Unified champion.
- Undisputed.
- Legend.

### Action Groups

- Train: conditioning, sparring, footwork, power, defense.
- Camp: hire coach, nutrition, recovery, strategy.
- Fight: book tune-up, contender fight, title eliminator, title fight.
- Business: sponsorship, media, purse negotiation.
- Health: rest, surgery, cut weight safely, retire.

### UX Feel

Use a fight-card layout.

Show upcoming opponent as a card:

- Record.
- Style.
- Threat.
- Purse.
- Reward.

### Risk Events

- Training injury.
- Bad weight cut.
- Rival trash talk.
- Last-minute opponent change.
- Controversial decision.
- Knockout loss.
- Promoter offer.

### Refined Presentation

Separate "Training Camp" from "Career Office".

Do not mix recovery, fight booking, and sponsorship in one button row.

## 7. Billionaire Expansion

### Fantasy

Billionaire gameplay should feel like capital allocation, institution control,
family legacy, public scrutiny, and world-scale choices.

### First Screen

Show:

- Net worth.
- Liquid cash.
- Holdings.
- Family office.
- Public scrutiny.
- Influence.
- Philanthropy.
- Current acquisition or project.
- Legacy goal.

### Primary Metrics

- Net Worth.
- Liquidity.
- Annual Cash Flow.
- Holdings Value.
- Reputation.
- Political Access.
- Public Scrutiny.
- Family Office Strength.
- Legacy Score.

### Progression Ladder

- High earner.
- First million.
- Founder/investor.
- Decamillionaire.
- Centimillionaire.
- Billionaire.
- Multi-billionaire.
- Family office dynasty.
- Global power broker.

### Action Groups

- Allocate: invest, acquire, sell, diversify.
- Lifestyle: yacht, jet, estate, art, security.
- Influence: lobbying, media ownership, political donation.
- Philanthropy: foundation, hospital wing, scholarship, climate fund.
- Family Office: hire CIO, tax counsel, succession plan.
- Legacy: museum, university, space project, city project.

### UX Feel

Make wealth feel strategic.

Avoid just making every button cost more.

High-end actions should have reputational and political consequences.

### Risk Events

- Market crash.
- Public scandal.
- Tax investigation.
- Family dispute.
- Acquisition lawsuit.
- Political backlash.
- Foundation fraud.

### Refined Presentation

Use a "family office" dashboard:

- Allocation pie/rows.
- Liquidity warning.
- Reputation/scrutiny strip.
- Big move opportunities.
- Legacy projects.

## 19. Feature Mixing Rules

Some features combine naturally:

- Crime + politics: corruption and protection.
- Vampire + royalty: immortal courts and blood dynasties.
- Boxing + fame: media pressure and sponsorships.
- Billionaire + politics: influence, donations, and public scrutiny.
- Monster + secret society: hidden factions and hunters.
- Royalty + dynasty: heirs, succession, and family legacy.

When mixing features:

- Keep one primary fantasy.
- Keep one primary progression ladder.
- Let secondary systems support the main loop.
- Avoid two separate screens fighting for attention.
- Surface cross-system consequences clearly.

Example:

If a billionaire becomes political, the Billionaire screen should show a
Political Access panel. Do not clone an entire politics hub unless politics has
become the main career.
